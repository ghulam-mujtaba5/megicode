# Megicode Brand Guidelines

## Core Positioning

Megicode helps founders and growing businesses turn AI, automation, and custom software into measurable business outcomes. Copy should answer "what is in it for me" quickly: saved time, clearer decisions, faster launch, stronger operations, and reduced build risk.

## Color System

- Primary blue: `#4573df` for trust, core buttons, links, badges, and key accents.
- Deep blue: `#2d4fa2` for gradients, depth, and serious enterprise tone.
- Action orange: `#ff9800` for high-salience consultation CTAs.
- Light surfaces: `#ffffff`, `#f8fafc`, and `#e8eaf6`.
- Dark surface: `#1d2127`.
- Body text: `#334155`; high-emphasis text: `#1d2127`.

Use blue and deep blue as the default Megicode identity. Use orange only for the most important action buttons so it stays meaningful.

## Typography

- Primary typeface: Open Sans.
- Use strong, outcome-first headlines.
- Avoid vague service copy such as "solutions for every business." Prefer concrete outcomes such as "Automate leads, replies, bookings, and repetitive workflows."
- Keep button labels verb-led: `Get a Free AI Consultation`, `Automate My Leads`, `Build My Platform`, `Grow My Traffic`.

## CTA Style

- Primary service CTAs use rounded pill buttons, strong contrast, and direct verbs.
- Consultation CTAs may use the orange accent to stand out.
- Secondary links should not compete visually with the main CTA.
- Service pages should show clear action points near the hero, mid-page, and final CTA section.

## Personalization Rules

- New visitors see best-selling services and trust signals.
- Returning visitors see a welcome-back message, previously viewed services, and a direct consultation shortcut.
- Recommendations should be behavior-aware but simple: use viewed services, current page, and referrer keywords before considering more complex ML.
- Always show local visitor time for scheduling context and reinforce remote-first global delivery.
